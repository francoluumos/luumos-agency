# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two buyer profiles, both decision-makers (founders, owners, ops leads), not end-users of a tool:

1. **Tech-savvy / AI-forward buyers** — companies actively looking for AI & automation and "agentic development" capability. More technical, innovation-led, evaluate on demonstrated competence.
2. **SMBs in high-billing service industries** — medical groups, architecture firms, medtech, planning/engineering practices. High hourly value, time-poor, drowning in manual operational work. They don't want "ERP" or "AI" as concepts; they want their operations to run with less friction.

Context of use: evaluating whether Luumos is a credible, expert partner worth a conversation. Often arriving from referral, search, or outreach; skeptical of agencies; short on time. The job to be done: *quickly decide "these people clearly know what they're doing — book a call."*

## Product Purpose

The marketing site for **Luumos** ([luumos.io](https://luumos.io)) — Franco Steiner's IT consulting firm and official **Odoo partner**, specialising in:

- **Odoo consulting & development** — implementations and custom development.
- **Agentic development systems for Odoo** — AI-agent-driven tooling to build and maintain Odoo faster.
- **AI & automation tools** — broader AI and workflow-automation for business operations.

The site exists to convert qualified prospects into booked consultations. Success = a steady flow of well-fit inbound leads (high-billing service SMBs + AI-forward buyers) who arrive already convinced of Luumos's competence and book a call. Positioning leans toward **AI & automation for operations**, with Odoo as the deep technical backbone — not an ERP-vendor pitch.

## Brand Personality

**Precise · premium · confident.** Engineering credibility meets high-end consultancy: quietly expensive, sharp, trustworthy. The voice is clear and direct, never hypey — it earns trust by demonstrating mastery, not by claiming it. Speaks to busy operators in their language (outcomes, time saved, fewer errors), not in jargon. Emotional goal: *relief and confidence* — "finally, someone serious who gets it."

## Anti-references

Explicitly avoid:

- **Crypto/AI hype** — neon, buzzword soup, fake-3D, "revolutionary" energy. We are the calm expert, not the hype machine.
- **Corporate SaaS boilerplate** — soulless enterprise templates, stock illustrations, say-nothing copy.
- **Cheap dev-shop / agency look** — Fiverr/template vibe, gradient blobs, undifferentiated "we build apps."
- **Generic Odoo-partner site** — templated, cluttered, feature-soup, ERP-vendor blandness.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the proof.** Craft, polish, and restraint are the argument — a flawlessly built site is itself evidence that Luumos builds flawless things. Practice what you preach.
2. **Show competence, don't claim it.** Concrete outcomes, real work, specific mechanisms over adjectives. Earn the "they clearly know what they're doing" reaction.
3. **Speak to the operator, not the engineer.** Lead with the buyer's operational pain and outcome; let the Odoo/AI depth be the credible "how," not the headline.
4. **Quiet confidence over volume.** Premium restraint — whitespace, one accent, precise type. Trust comes from calm, not from shouting.
5. **Every path leads to a conversation.** The whole experience is engineered toward one low-friction action: book a consult.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic structure, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Relevant given medical/professional-services buyers who may hold their own accessibility expectations.
